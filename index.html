About me

Educator · Engineer · Researcher

I am a faculty member in the Department of Mechanical Engineering at the College of Science and Technology (CST), Royal University of Bhutan, based in Phuentsholing. My work spans teaching, curriculum development, academic administration, and applied research in manufacturing technologies.

My primary teaching areas include Computer Integrated Manufacturing (CIM), CNC Programming, Mechanical Measurement & Metrology, and Computer Graphics — all delivered to undergraduate mechanical engineering students using industry-standard tools including Siemens SinuTrain (Sinumerik 840D sl).

I am deeply interested in developing rich, interactive learning materials that connect engineering theory to practical industrial application, preparing students for Bhutan's evolving industrial landscape.


Research interests

  • CNC/CIM technologies and digital manufacturing
  • Sustainable engineering systems (rainwater harvesting, green infrastructure)
  • Smart industrial automation and Industry 4.0
  • Engineering education and curriculum innovation
  • Digital twins and smart manufacturing systems

Teaching

Courses & Modules

MDD303

Computer Integrated Manufacturing

CIM concepts, CAD/CAM integration, automation strategies, Purdue model, Industry 4.0, and CNC programming using Siemens SinuTrain.

Year 3 · Undergraduate Core Module
MDD303

CNC Programming

G-code and ShopMill/ShopTurn programming on Sinumerik 840D sl; turning, milling, fixed cycles, practical lab worksheets.

Year 3 · Undergraduate Lab Module
MMT

Mechanical Measurement & Metrology

Measurement concepts, linear & angular measurement, limits/fits/tolerances, comparators, surface and gear inspection.

Year 2–3 · Undergraduate Core Module
CG

Computer Graphics for Engineers

2D/3D geometric transformations, surface & solid modelling, synthetic curves (Hermite, Bézier, B-spline, NURBS), MATLAB labs.

Year 2–3 · Undergraduate Core Module
FEM

Finite Element Method

Fundamentals of FEM, element types, mesh generation, boundary conditions, and engineering applications using simulation software.

Year 3–4 · Undergraduate Elective
PRW402

On-the-Job Training (OJT)

Industrial placement supervision and report evaluation across Bhutanese industries including hydropower, manufacturing, and fabrication.

Year 4 · Undergraduate Practicum
